Eraseer _ (visuals) Sadie Meadow, (sound) Violet W & Chuck Roth [~15 min]

“Eraseer” is a work-in-progress multimedia noise ensemble piece. This iteration of the Eraseer Ensemblle features Sadie Meadow performing video synthesis. Chuck Roth & Violet W will perform a timbralsensory duet. Experience spiritual paralysis. Contains strobing images.

Violet W formed the Eraseer Ensemblle is a and spent 10 of the 12 months of 2022 riding a prevost charter bus in a recursive spiral around north america which facilitated conditions for her chance meeting with Eraseer. the rest is already has happened. she has released music and also performed labor for money. the current history is here: https://violet0404.xyz/

That you had not made me a woman _ Sarah Lou Haddad [2.5 min]

This video explores the difference between men's and women’s daily morning blessings in the Jewish prayer of Shacharit. By juxtaposing the feminine body with the literal words of the prayer, I attempt to address the feminine Jewish body and its place within the Jewish religion.

Systole _ Emma Penrose, Zack Shorrosh, Sandro Gomes [2 min]

Systole (2022) is a 2 minute silent film shot in chronological order on one 100’ daylight spool of 16mm Ektachrome film. It was developed & spliced by hand, then spooled onto a reel with films by fellow Casa do Xisto artists in residence for its premiere screening at the Curtas Vila do Conde International Film Festival. This film was made possible by Casa do Xisto residency in Barcelos, Portugal.

Lifeforce _ Cherry Nin [15 min]

In Life Force, Cherry Nin utilizes alchemical methods of filmmaking to weave together an apocalyptic articulation of sexuality and nature, as it exists in a post-industrial U.S. city landscape—i.e. the idea of “life force” as intrinsically queer, in decay, dead, and situated within multiple trajectories of power and matter. Though the repercussion of these trajectories is separation, a relentless reaching towards each other and towards life persists.

Vacuumism _ Alex Killgore [7 min]

When all hope is seemingly gone, the Vacuum becomes solitude. Weaved into the darkness of the void, there is a shimmer of light that has manifested into The Self. Can We usher in a new era of art? Are We able to shed the skin of the Past?

Vaccuumism examines the Now as a opportunity for true unfettered growth. For too long We’ve needed something New. Through Our own collectively imposed self isolation, the possibilities are suddenly infinite.
